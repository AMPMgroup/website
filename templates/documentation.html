Documentation and Help




Antibodies are heavy proteins that are released by mature B-cells, that aid in identifying and capturing antigens that invade the bodies of most vertebrae. The antibodies are antigen specifc, due to their highly variable loops that are known as the antibodies' Complimentarity Determining Regions (CDRs). There are 4 chains in every antibody- two heavy and two light chains, and there are 3 CDRs on the distal ends of each of the chain- H1, H2, H3 and L1, L2 and L3.

Antigens are described as a class of objects that are considered 'foreign' to the immune system of an organism. These objects range from viruses and bacteria, to worms. Sometimes molecules and secretions released from parts of the body are also considered as antigens.

As the term antigen is used to refer to something specific from an extremely diverse class, it is crucial that the the information be organised and classified in a manner that is both easily accessed and viewed.

Networks are used for better visualisation of interactions between various antibodies and antigens. In this database, networks are used as a tool to find a query's corresponding antigen/antibody.
